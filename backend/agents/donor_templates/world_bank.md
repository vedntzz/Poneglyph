# World Bank Quarterly Progress Report — Section Template

SYNTHETIC DATA — Reference template for Poneglyph's Drafter agent.
Based on typical World Bank Implementation Status and Results Report (ISR) conventions.

---

## Conventions

- **Tone**: Formal, evidence-based, precise. No marketing language.
- **Person**: Third person. "The project team" not "we."
- **Numbers**: Always precise. "187 women" not "nearly 200 women."
- **Dates**: Full format. "March 1, 2026" not "last month."
- **Targets**: Always reference the Results Framework indicator ID.
- **Structure**: Context → Progress → Challenges/Risks → Next Steps.

## Section structure

### [Section Title — maps to a Results Framework indicator]

**Target**: [Indicator ID] — [target description with number and unit]

**Progress**: [2-3 sentences stating measurable progress against the target.
Every number cited to a specific evidence source.]

**Evidence basis**: [1-2 sentences describing what evidence supports the
progress claim — field forms, attendance sheets, site photos, meeting records.]

**Gaps and risks**: [1-2 sentences on what evidence is missing, what districts
or time periods are uncovered, or what risks could affect target achievement.]

**Next steps**: [1-2 sentences on planned activities for the next quarter.]

### Output 3.2: Women's Post-Harvest Management Training

**Target**: 200 women trained in post-harvest management techniques across
target villages by March 31, 2026.

**Progress**: As of March 1, 2026, 187 women have completed PHM training
across 9 villages in Sagar district [mtg-002]. The highest single-session
turnout was 47 participants in Gumla village [mtg-002]. Training materials
are in final review following the Gumla pilot session [cmt-005].

**Evidence basis**: Progress figures are derived from meeting minutes of
the Q1 Progress Review held on March 1, 2026 [mtg-002]. No independent
field evidence (attendance sheets, photos) has been submitted to the
project binder for cross-verification.

**Gaps and risks**: Three of the original 12 target villages have not yet
received training [mtg-001, mtg-002]. FarmTrac uploads are complete for
7 of 9 sessions; 2 sessions lack attendance sheets [mtg-002]. The absence
of field evidence means progress cannot be independently verified ahead
of the April review mission.

**Next steps**: Complete training in remaining villages by March 31, 2026.
Upload all outstanding attendance sheets and photos to FarmTrac. Prepare
evidence packages for the World Bank review mission scheduled for April 2026.
